Retail Hubs Bringing Big Crowds Also Attract Mass Shooters

Allen Premium Outlets Shuttered Nearly a Month After Mass Shooting

Story by Candace Carlisle, Nicole Shih

The largest U.S. landlord has re-opened a Texas outlet mall temporary closed for weeks following the nation's second deadliest shooting this year where eight people, including three children, were killed nearly a month ago.

The shooting on May 6, where eight people were killed and seven were hospitalized when an armed gunman pulled up in a car and started shooting at pedestrians and shoppers in the parking lot, led to Indianapolis-based Simon Property Group temporarily closing one of the busiest regional shopping destinations in Dallas-Fort Worth. The weeks-long closure of the 100% leased 548,000-square-foot outlet mall affected about 120 retailers and thousands of jobs.

There are more mass shootings in retail centers than anywhere else when broken down by property type, according to the latest data from the Violence Protection Project Research Center, a nonprofit group seeking to reduce violence through research to inform public policy. About 50% of the mass shooters identified by the nonprofit group legally purchased the weapons they later used to kill others.

In the Allen Premium Outlets shooting, the gunman legally purchased the weapons and allegedly identified himself as a white supremacist with neo-Nazi ideology, according to multiple media reports. The shooting in Allen took place less than a year after a school shooting in Uvalde, Texas, where 19 children and two adults were killed at Robb Elementary School by a teenage shooter. It was also only months after another shooting at an El Paso mall that was all too reminiscent of another shooting in the West Texas city at a nearby Walmart in 2019.

In attracting crowds, retailers and landlords have had to prepare stakeholders for the possibility of a mass shooting and the lasting impact to not only the community, but the underlying real estate. For Simon Property Group, which owns Greenwood Park Mall in Indiana where a shooter killed three people last year, the landlord with more than 200 retail properties in the United States has told investors this is a risk to its business, such as decreased sales, revenue and overall asset value.
